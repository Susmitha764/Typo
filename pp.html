Typography & Scripts

Playfair Display

A sophisticated transitional serif with high contrast and delicate hairlines.

Garamond

An elegant old-style serif typeface with a timeless quality.

Baskerville

A transitional serif with organic forms and vertical stress.

Bodoni

A modern serif with extreme contrast between thick and thin strokes.

Helvetica

The quintessential neutral sans-serif with clean, no-nonsense lines.

Futura

A geometric sans-serif based on simple geometric shapes.

Gill Sans

A humanist sans-serif with subtle variation in stroke width.

Roboto

A modern, approachable sans-serif with mechanical skeletons.

Typography has a rich history dating back to the invention of movable type by Johannes Gutenberg in the 15th century. The development of typefaces has evolved through several distinct periods:

  • Blackletter (1400s): The first typefaces modeled after handwritten manuscripts.
  • Humanist (late 1400s): More readable forms inspired by Roman inscriptions.

Kerning: The adjustment of space between individual letter pairs.

Leading: The vertical space between lines of text.

X-height: The height of lowercase letters, excluding ascenders and descenders.

Serif: The small decorative strokes at the ends of letterforms.
